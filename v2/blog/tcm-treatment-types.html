Understanding TCM Treatment Types:
Acupuncture, Herbal Medicine & Manipulation

Acupuncture Herbal Medicine Beginner Guide

When people say they are "going for TCM," they often mean something different each time. One patient means acupuncture. Another means picking up a herbal formula. A third means a session of tui na massage. Traditional Chinese Medicine is not one therapy — it is a system built from several core modalities that work together to restore balance in the body.

Understanding which modality does what — and when each is most appropriate — helps you have a better conversation with your practitioner and set realistic expectations for treatment.

"TCM treats the root, not just the branch. Each modality is a different way of accessing the same underlying system."

— Mr. Siew Kuen Xian, Co-Founder & Head Practitioner, HansMed
The three core TCM modalities — often used together in a single treatment plan · 三大療法相輔相成

Acupuncture · 針灸

qi 氣（氣的流動）· meridians 經絡（經脈）· acupoints 穴位（針刺點）

Acupuncture involves the insertion of very fine, sterile needles at specific points (穴位, acupoints) along the body's meridian channels (經絡). According to TCM theory, these channels carry qi (氣) — the body's vital energy — and disruptions in this flow manifest as pain, illness, or emotional imbalance.

From a modern clinical perspective, acupuncture is understood to stimulate the nervous system, influence pain signalling pathways, and promote the release of endorphins and anti-inflammatory compounds. It is one of the most extensively researched complementary therapies globally, with evidence supporting its use for musculoskeletal pain, headaches, stress, and various internal conditions.

When Acupuncture Works Well

Neck and back pain, migraines, tennis elbow, insomnia, menstrual irregularities, fertility support, anxiety, post-operative recovery. Most patients report mild soreness at needle sites — this typically resolves within hours.

Herbal Medicine · 中藥

constitution 體質（個人體質）· formula 方劑（複方配方）· decoction 煎劑（水煎藥）

Chinese herbal medicine draws from a materia medica of over 400 commonly used substances — roots, bark, leaves, seeds, and minerals — each with documented therapeutic properties developed across two millennia of clinical observation.

The critical distinction from over-the-counter supplements is that herbal formulas are prescribed to your specific constitution (體質), not to a diagnosis in isolation. Two patients presenting with the same complaint may receive entirely different formulas, because their underlying constitutional patterns are different. This is not imprecision — it is the core philosophy of TCM.

At HansMed, all herbal prescriptions are written by a licensed practitioner following your consultation. Herbs are sourced from verified suppliers with full labelling in Chinese (漢字), pinyin, and English.

When Herbal Medicine Works Well

Digestive conditions, chronic fatigue, hormonal imbalance, sleep disorders, skin conditions (eczema, acne), immune support, menstrual health, recovery from illness. Herbal medicine is particularly well-suited to conditions that are chronic or constitutional in nature.

Manipulation · 推拿 & 拔罐

tui na 推拿（推拿按摩）· cupping 拔罐（拔火罐）· gua sha 刮痧

Tui na (推拿) is a form of therapeutic bodywork that works along the meridian channels and acupoints through pressing, rolling, kneading, and stretching techniques. It is distinct from Western massage in that it targets specific channels rather than muscle groups alone, and is always guided by TCM diagnostic principles.

Cupping (拔罐) creates suction on the skin surface to improve local circulation, release muscle tension, and draw out pathogens from deeper tissue layers. The characteristic circular marks left by cupping are not bruises — they reflect the release of stagnated blood and lymph from the tissue.

Both are particularly effective for musculoskeletal conditions and are commonly combined with acupuncture in the same session for compounding effect.

How the three work together · 三法合一

In practice, most treatment plans combine modalities. A patient presenting with lower back pain, for example, might receive acupuncture at local and distal points, a tui na session targeting the lumbar region, and a short-term herbal formula to address the underlying constitutional pattern — perhaps kidney yang deficiency (腎陽虛) — driving the condition.

Your practitioner will determine the appropriate combination at your first consultation, based on your constitution, the nature of your complaint, and your personal circumstances.

What to expect at your first consultation · 首次問診

  • Detailed symptom and lifestyle history — what, when, and how long
  • Tongue examination (舌診) — colour, coating, shape, moisture
  • Pulse taking (脈診) — quality, rate, and character at three positions
  • Constitution assessment — your dominant pattern and imbalances
  • Treatment plan — which modalities, how often, expected timeline

Both in-person and video consultations at HansMed include all of the above. For video sessions, tongue examination is conducted via photo upload and your practitioner will guide you through pulse description.
