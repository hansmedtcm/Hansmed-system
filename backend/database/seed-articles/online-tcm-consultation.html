When patients first hear that HansMed offers video consultations, the most common reaction is scepticism. "Can a TCM doctor really assess me through a screen?" It is a fair question — and the honest answer is: for many conditions, yes. For others, no. Understanding the difference is what separates good teleconsult from bad.

"The majority of what I need to know from a patient — their constitution, their patterns, their tongue — I can assess thoroughly through a well-structured video consultation."

— Mr. Lim Gao Hong, Co-Founder & Head Practitioner, HansMed

The myth: you cannot diagnose without touching the patient · 誤解：不觸診就無法診斷

This assumption comes from conflating TCM diagnosis with Western physical examination. In TCM, the four diagnostic methods are observation (望, wàng), listening/smelling (聞, wén), inquiry (問, wèn), and palpation (切, qiē). The first three — which include tongue examination, voice quality, and detailed questioning — are all fully accessible via video.

Pulse taking (脈診) is the one method that genuinely requires physical contact. For video consultations, practitioners guide patients through a self-assessment of their pulse characteristics (rate, strength, and rhythm), which provides meaningful supplementary information alongside tongue and symptom analysis.

For the majority of conditions — especially internal, constitutional, and chronic presentations — this is entirely sufficient for an accurate assessment and a well-formulated treatment plan.

What works well online · 適合線上問診的情況

The conditions most suited to teleconsult are precisely those that are most common among Malaysians seeking TCM: stress and burnout, digestive discomfort, sleep disorders, fatigue, hormonal imbalance, skin conditions, and chronic pain that has already been physically assessed.

The tongue provides an extraordinary amount of information. Colour, coating thickness, shape, moisture, and the presence of teeth marks or cracks each correspond to specific constitutional patterns and organ imbalances — all clearly visible in a well-lit photograph. At HansMed, every tongue photo submitted for an AI wellness assessment is also reviewed by a licensed practitioner before any insight reaches you.

Your practitioner will advise you at the start of every session which format is appropriate · 醫師將在問診開始時告知您最合適的方式

What requires in-person attendance · 須親身到診的情況

Honesty matters here. If your condition requires acupuncture, tui na manipulation, or cupping, those treatments cannot be delivered remotely. Similarly, complex musculoskeletal presentations — a suspected disc herniation, post-surgical recovery, or acute sports injury — benefit significantly from hands-on physical assessment before any treatment plan is finalised.

Your HansMed practitioner will tell you clearly at the beginning of every video consultation if they feel an in-person assessment is warranted. We do not recommend continuing with a teleconsult if it would compromise the quality of your care.

And to state this plainly: if you are experiencing chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden neurological symptoms, or any other emergency, please call 999 immediately. HansMed is not an emergency service.

The HansMed difference · HansMed 的不同之處

Many wellness apps offer AI-driven assessments with no practitioner involvement. HansMed is built differently. Every consultation — online or in-person — is conducted by a licensed, T&CM Act 2016 registered practitioner. Our AI wellness tools are educational supplements, not replacements for clinical judgement. Every AI report is reviewed and approved by a practitioner before it reaches you.

This matters because it protects you. An AI tool reading your tongue photo has no knowledge of your medication history, your past diagnoses, or the subtleties that a trained practitioner notices in a real consultation. We use technology to make care more accessible — not to replace the clinical relationship that makes TCM effective.
